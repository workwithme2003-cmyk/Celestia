Mercury

Terrestrial Innermost No Atmosphere

The smallest planet in our solar system and nearest to the Sun. Its lack of atmosphere causes extreme temperature swings — boiling hot days and freezing cold nights, swinging over 600°C between them. Mercury completes an orbit every 88 Earth days.

Terrestrial Hottest Retrograde

Venus spins slowly in the opposite direction from most planets. Covered by thick, toxic clouds of sulfuric acid, a runaway greenhouse effect makes it hotter than Mercury — despite being farther from the Sun. Surface pressure is 90× Earth's.

Terrestrial Our Home Life-Bearing

Our home planet and the only known world to harbor life. 71% of its surface is liquid ocean. A dynamic magnetic field, active plate tectonics, and a protective ozone layer make it uniquely alive in a solar system of extremes.
